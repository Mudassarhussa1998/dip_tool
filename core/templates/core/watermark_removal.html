Watermark Removal — Theory
What is a Watermark?

A watermark is a visible or semi-transparent overlay (text, logo, pattern) added to an image to indicate ownership or prevent unauthorised use.

Detection Strategies
  • Bright/Dark threshold: Pixels above (or below) a luminance threshold are flagged as watermark.
  • Edge-based: Strong edge clusters (Canny) indicate text or logo boundaries.
  • Saturation: Semi-transparent grey/white overlays have low colour saturation.
  • Frequency domain: Periodic watermarks produce distinct peaks in the FFT magnitude spectrum.
Inpainting (Telea / Navier-Stokes)

Inpainting reconstructs the masked region by propagating texture from the surrounding unmasked pixels.

Telea uses a fast marching method; Navier-Stokes uses fluid-dynamics equations for smoother fills.

Frequency Notch Filtering

For periodic watermarks, the FFT spectrum shows isolated bright peaks. Zeroing those peaks (notch filter) and inverting the transform removes the periodic pattern:

$\hat{F}(u,v) = F(u,v) \cdot (1 - N(u,v))$

where $N(u,v)$ is the notch mask.

Median / Gaussian Fill

Simpler alternatives: replace masked pixels with the local median or Gaussian-blurred value. Fast but less accurate for complex backgrounds.
